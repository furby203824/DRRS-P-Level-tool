Methodology

How P-Level is calculated. Reference for manual computation or audit. Sections are expanded by default; click any heading to collapse.

01 What P-Level Measures

P-Level is the personnel readiness rating for your unit per MCO 3000.13B DRRS Marine Corps Readiness Reporting paragraph 7c. It is the lower of two percentages: Personnel Strength and Critical MOS. The lower (worse) band wins. P-1 is best, P-4 is worst. P-5 reserves for non-reportable status. P-6 only by direction of HQMC PP&O.

02 The Two Formulas
Personnel Strength
((Assigned + Attached) − (Detached + Non-Deployable + IA/JIA)) / Structure Strength × 100
Critical MOS
((Crit Assigned + Attached) − (Crit Detached + Crit Non-Dep + Crit IA/JIA)) / Crit Structure Strength × 100

Same shape, different population. “Assigned + Attached” is the total unit headcount on books. Detached, Non-Deployable, and IA/JIA are subtracted from that total. Non-Deployable includes DLC = N and (per unit policy) DLC = L. Whichever metric scores lower wins. Period.

Critical MOS fill is computed by greedy matching of qualified Marines to critical billets (BMOS-primary then PMOS-secondary, exact grade preferred then ±1). Detail in section A.

Critical MOS thresholds are 5 points lower at every band because rare skills are harder to fill. A unit at 85% critical fill is still P-1 critical even though it would be P-2 on personnel strength.

04 Manual Calculation — Six Steps
  1. Pull T/O Authorized. From TFSMS, sum all authorized billets for your UIC. This is your personnel strength denominator. Pull the critical MOS subset using the unit type's critical MOS list. Both numbers stay fixed during the report period.
  2. Pull the Roster. From AAA via Cognos, get every Marine assigned to or attached to your UIC. Include detached Marines so you have full visibility. Exclude contractors and civilians unless your T/O authorizes them.
  3. Classify Each Marine. Tag each Marine with one DRRS state: ASSIGNED, ATTACHED, DETACHED, IA, or JIA. For ASSIGNED and ATTACHED Marines, also flag deployability using the DLC code. Detached, IA, and JIA Marines reduce the numerator by removing them from the pool entirely.
  4. Compute the Numerator. Start with Assigned plus Attached (total unit headcount on books). Subtract Detached, Non-Deployable, IA, and JIA. Non-Deployable includes DLC = N and (per unit policy) DLC = L. Per MCO 3000.13B para 7c.
  5. Divide and Multiply. Numerator divided by T/O Authorized, times 100. Round to one decimal. Repeat the entire calculation for the Critical MOS subset using the critical roster and critical T/O denominator.
  6. Map to Band and Take the Lower. Map the Personnel Strength percentage to a band using the PS table. Map the Critical MOS percentage using the CM table. Your final P-Level is the lower (worse) of the two bands. Note which one is binding for your remarks.
05 Critical MOS Fill Rules

Per MCO 3000.13B paragraph 7c, the rules for whether a Marine counts as filling a critical billet:

BIC First
The Marine must be assigned to a Billet Identification Code on the unit's T/O.
BMOS Primary
If the Billet MOS is on the critical list, the fill counts toward critical MOS.
PMOS Secondary
If the BMOS is not critical but the Marine's PMOS is critical, the Marine fills the critical PMOS gap rather than their actual billet.
±1 Grade
A Marine within one paygrade of the authorized billet grade counts as a valid fill. An E5 in an authorized E6 billet counts. An E5 in an authorized E7 billet does not.
No Double Count
A single Marine fills exactly one billet. If their BMOS is critical, the BMOS fill is the only one credited.
Qualified
The Marine must hold the required MOS qualification, not the title alone.
06 Who Gets Excluded
  • Contractors. Never count toward T/O strength or critical fill.
  • Civilian employees. Excluded unless the T/O authorizes them.
  • IRR Marines on inactive status.
  • Marines with EAS within 90 days. Typically flagged Non-Deployable per DLC code B.
  • Cadets and trainees not yet T/O assigned.

A How This App Implements It

Notes on where the tool's behavior maps to the methodology above:

  • Numerator subtraction. The app counts Detached, IA, and JIA via DRRSStatus and excludes them from the Assigned+Attached pool. Non-Deployable (DeployableFlag = N) is subtracted only among Assigned+Attached to avoid the double-count the methodology calls out.
  • Limited duty. DeployableFlag = L Marines are subtracted from the numerator by default (conservative read of “fully deployable”). Toggle the Unit Policy checkbox on the upload card to count them toward effective strength instead.
  • Critical MOS matching. Two-stage greedy fill: first BMOS (Marine in seat for a critical billet), then PMOS (qualified Marine credited toward an unfilled critical billet). Within each stage, exact paygrade is preferred; ±1 grade is the fallback. The same Marine is never counted twice.
  • Paygrade ladders. The ±1 rule applies on the same E/W/O ladder. An O3 cannot fill an E8 billet even though the numeric distance is small.
  • Contractors. Rows with Service of CTR / CONTRACTOR are filtered out before calculation.
  • Rounding. Percentages display to one decimal place. Band lookup uses the unrounded value.
